IS&T

The Redesign of the IS&T Website was a project that I worked on while in the Interaction Design Practicum Class here at UVU with Tressa Hopkinson. In this class, we were tasked with helping the IS&T department do research in regards to the redesign of their website. We worked as teams and worked on creating a design that would best help a specific persona that we chose.

What were we doing?

As we started working on this project, it wasn't very clear what the overall goal of it was. We were told we were working on a redesign, but that we shouldn't focus on the redesign and more focus on the research on our specific personas. And so, that is what we spent the most time on throughout the process.

Our Persona

The Persona that we chose was that of the prospective master's student. We then worked on creating an imaginary person who would become the generic master's student that we could have in mind as we did research and the redesign.

Researching and Working as a team

With our audience directly in mind, we started by doing some quantitative research. We needed to reach people and understand questions like why are they wanting to become a master's student, what would they look for in a website, and those things that are most important on a website for them. So, we created a survey and sent it off to anyone and everyone that we could find who is a masters student, was a masters student, or is a prospective masters student. And the results didn't really surprise us. Most People who were looking at receiving a masters degree, were looking at it for mainly 1 of 3 reasons. 1. In order to further progress higher up in their field of work. 2. In order to receive a higher paying salary/job. Or 3. Looking at becoming more specialized in their current field. Below you can see some of our results. From there we wanted to know some information about the current IS&T site, and so we did some qualitative research by doing some eye tracking. We wanted to see what on the current site was working when giving a user a task, vs. what was drawing their attention away from it as they were working on the same task. It was here where we found some issues. The biggest gripe that was had with the current website was the landing page was cluttered and didn't have the information people were expecting it to have, and overall the overall navigation didn't end up leading people to where it was supposed to. So, that is where we started. We went to other department websites and found their navigation options, we looked into landing designs and overall pictures and finally went into the wireframes. From wire frames, we then went onto surface comps and prototyping. But this is about where we hit our first road bump. You see about halfway through this process, my father passed away. And my focus completely disappeared along with my last few functioning brain cells. At this time, I had been working on a lot of the presentation of the research we had performed and was gearing up to continue with eye tracking. But with this major life event, tasks needed to be reassigned amongst our team, and we needed to get stuff fingered out. With the wonderful team that I was working with, we were able to keep on moving. Slower than before, but moving nonetheless. When I was ready to get back to work, we were starting on the prototyping. Getting a design ready and active so that we could do some usability testing. But it was at this time, when our next road bump showed up. The IS&T department had a group of web designers and developers working on new designs in the background while we were doing our research and updated their website with a new design as we were almost finishing up. With that, it was really hard to stay focused with our designs. Knowing that in the end, none of it really mattered anymore was a little sad and demotivating, but we did our best to push through and move on with those usability tests. With our Usability testing, we found that for the most part, the new designs made since, and working slightly better than the original website.And with that the project was finished.

Final Product

As we finished up, we did have a successfully redesigned website, and plenty of research to show why we chose to do what we did. Even though Our designs were no longer applicable, it was good to know that they were a sign of our hard work, and effort.

Looking Back

In the end this was a good learning experience for me. This was not only the first time that I had really worked for a client, but also the first time that I had really worked in a team. But, there are a few things that I would change if I had the opportunity to go back. I personally feel that we didn't fully understand our demographic and persona. Even with all the research, I still feel like there is a major part of the demographic we had missed. I would love to have been able to get more research, and then fully understand what our demographic was looking for, and what they were wanting with their work.
